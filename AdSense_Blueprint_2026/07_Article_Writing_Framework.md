# Phase 7: Article Writing Framework

This standard operating procedure (SOP) ensures every article meets the 2026 standards for Google's Helpful Content System and EEAT requirements.

## 1. Title, Meta, and URL Rules
- **URL**: Keep it to 3-5 words. Use the exact target keyword. No stop words. (e.g., `/notion-crm-template`).
- **Meta Title**: Must include the exact keyword at the beginning, a power word, and the current year if relevant. Keep under 60 characters.
- **Meta Description**: 150-160 characters. State the problem, the solution, and end with a Call to Action (CTA).

## 2. Introduction Formula (APP Strategy)
- **Align**: Immediately agree with the reader's problem or search intent in the first sentence. Do not write generic history ("Since the dawn of time, software has...").
- **Present**: Present the exact solution you are going to provide in this article.
- **Prove (EEAT)**: Explain *why* you are qualified to teach this (e.g., "After testing 15 CRMs over the past 3 years at my agency...").

## 3. Heading Hierarchy & Semantic Keywords
- **H1**: The main title (Only one per page).
- **H2**: Main subtopics (These should answer the primary intent).
- **H3**: Elaborations on H2s.
- **Semantic Keywords**: Do not stuff exact match keywords. Include LSI (Latent Semantic Indexing) keywords naturally.

## 4. Search Intent Matching & Formatting
- If the intent is a list, format it as a numbered list immediately.
- Use **Comparison Boxes** for commercial reviews (Pros/Cons lists).
- Add a "Key Takeaways" box at the top of articles longer than 1,500 words.

## 5. Experience Signals (Crucial for 2026)
- **Original Research**: Conduct polls, analyze data, or share personal analytics.
- **Original Media**: Do not rely purely on stock photos. Use custom screenshots with annotations (arrows, highlights).
- **Expert Quotes**: If you are not the expert, quote one. Link to their LinkedIn or authoritative site.
- **First-Person Language**: Use "I" and "We." "In our testing, we found..."

## 6. Pre-Publication Checklist
- [ ] Keyword in H1, first paragraph, and 1-2 H2s.
- [ ] At least 3 internal links to other relevant articles.
- [ ] At least 2 external links to highly authoritative, non-competing sources (e.g., official docs, Wikipedia, academic journals).
- [ ] All images have descriptive alt text and are compressed.
- [ ] FAQ section included at the bottom addressing 3-4 'People Also Ask' queries.
- [ ] Grammarly/Hemingway check (Aim for 8th-grade reading level).
- [ ] Formatting is highly scannable (short paragraphs, bullet points, bold text).
- [ ] EEAT verification (Author bio is correct, experience is demonstrated).
