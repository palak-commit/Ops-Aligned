# Phase 10: Long-Term Growth & Scaling Strategy (Day 90 to $1,000/mo)

Reaching the first $1,000/month in AdSense revenue usually requires between 30,000 to 100,000 monthly pageviews, depending on the niche's RPM. This requires scaling beyond the initial 30 articles.

## 1. The 6-Month to 12-Month Roadmap

### Months 4-6: Topical Authority Dominance
- **Goal**: 100 published articles.
- **Strategy**: Completely exhaust your first two content clusters. If your cluster is "Zapier," write every possible permutation of Zapier tutorials before moving to a new software.
- **Action**: Increase publishing velocity to 3-5 articles per week using the defined Article Framework.
- **Monetization**: Introduce high-ticket affiliate links alongside AdSense. Ensure AdSense auto-ads are optimized (limit ad load to avoid destroying UX).

### Months 7-12: Traffic Diversification & Expansion
- **Goal**: 250 published articles.
- **Strategy**: Start building an email list. Offer a free digital product (e.g., "50 Notion Templates") in exchange for emails.
- **Action**: Begin updating decaying content (articles over 6 months old that aren't ranking). 
- **Traffic Diversification**: Repurpose top-performing articles into YouTube videos or Pinterest graphics to reduce reliance on Google organic search.

## 2. Advanced Scaling Tactics

- **Content Refresh Schedule**: Every quarter, identify pages ranking in positions 11-20. Update these pages with fresh information, new images, and better formatting to push them to Page 1.
- **Google Update Recovery Strategy**: If hit by a core update:
  1. Do not panic or make massive changes immediately. Wait for the update rollout to finish.
  2. Audit for thin content. Prune (delete or redirect) articles with 0 traffic over 6 months.
  3. Strengthen EEAT. Update the author bio, add more original data, and remove excessive affiliate links.

# Final Checklists

## Technical SEO Checklist
- [ ] Core Web Vitals passing (LCP < 2.5s).
- [ ] HTTPS forced across the whole site.
- [ ] XML sitemap submitted in Google Search Console.
- [ ] `robots.txt` optimized (not blocking important folders).
- [ ] No duplicate H1 tags.
- [ ] All URLs are clean, lowercase, and use hyphens.
- [ ] Schema markup properly implemented and validating without errors in Rich Results Test.

## On-Page SEO Checklist
- [ ] Target keyword in URL, Meta Title, and first 100 words.
- [ ] Title is compelling and matches Search Intent.
- [ ] Proper H2/H3 structure used. No skipped heading levels (e.g., H2 jumping to H4).
- [ ] Content answers the primary query immediately (no fluff).
- [ ] Internal links to pillar pages included.
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources included.
- [ ] Images compressed and include Alt text.

## Common SEO Mistakes to Avoid
1. **Keyword Cannibalization**: Writing 5 different articles targeting the exact same keyword intent. (Instead, consolidate into one massive guide).
2. **Ignoring Search Intent**: Writing a "how-to" guide when Google is ranking e-commerce product pages for that keyword.
3. **Over-Optimizing Anchor Text**: Using the exact keyword for every internal link. Use natural variations.
4. **Publishing and Praying**: Not manually requesting indexing in GSC for important new posts.
5. **Set and Forget**: Never updating old content. Google loves fresh content.
